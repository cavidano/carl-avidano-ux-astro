## Challenge

Cheetah Conservation Fund (CCF) needed a modern, responsive website that was easy to update and maintain while preserving their recognizable brand identity. Their existing site had an outdated, photo-based logo that was not scalable, and the content structure made information difficult to find for visitors.

## Solution

I led the redesign and redevelopment of cheetah.org, including a scalable logo refresh, clearer site architecture, responsive page layouts, and WordPress tools that helped CCF staff manage content across the site.

## Results

In June 2019, cheetah.org launched as a more flexible and engaging platform for supporters around the world, with stronger donation pathways, clearer content, and a visual system CCF could continue using across web, email, and print.

</CaseStudyOverview>

## Logo refresh

CCF needed a new logo to replace their outdated, photo-based version that wasn't scalable and couldn't work as a single color. Since their logo was so recognizable, a complete redesign wasn't an option. I created a simple vector version of the running cheetah and adjusted the typography to feel more contemporary.

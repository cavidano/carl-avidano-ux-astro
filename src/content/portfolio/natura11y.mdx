## Why I built Natura11y

I love building things from scratch. I also understand the power and benefits of established frameworks. As I worked on increasingly complex websites, I found myself heavily customizing third-party frameworks  to meet my clients needs, and personal design philosophy. While frameworks like Bootstrap offer advantages and can accelerate development, I often found that the amount of customization required outweighed the benefits for the types of projects I was building.

At the same time, I wanted to deepen my understanding of front-end development, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React through real-world application rather than isolated tutorials or throwaway exercises. What began as a personal learning project gradually evolved into Natura11y, an accessibility-first design and development ecosystem built through years of experimentation, iteration, and client work.

Rather than setting out to replace existing frameworks, my goal was to create a system aligned with my own philosophy: code should be minimal, accessibility should be built in from the start, design and development should share the same foundation, and common patterns should be easy to implement without excessive customization.

## An end-to-end design system

Over time, Natura11y has grown into an end-to-end design system. It's been used in real world project such as Cheetah Conservation Fund, Visionlearning, and others. 

Naturally supports every part of the project life cycle: from low-fidelity ideation and design exploration to production-ready code.

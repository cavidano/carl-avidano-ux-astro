## NYC Office of Technology and Innovation

As a Senior UX Designer with the New York City Office of Technology and Innovation (OTI), I collaborated across teams and agencies to design and deliver digital services used by millions of New Yorkers and the city staff who support them.

My work included public websites, emergency alerts, digital records services, and internal tools for managing city infrastructure. Each project had different goals, but all focused on making complex city services easier to use.

</CaseStudyOverview>

<TextBlock>

## Materials for the Arts

Materials for the Arts (MFTA), located in Queens, is NYC's primary creative reuse center. It supports arts and education by giving donated materials to arts nonprofits, public schools, and city agencies across the five boroughs.

</TextBlock>

<TextBlock>

### Public website and donation management portal

When I joined the project, development had been stalled for nearly two years. I worked with the project manager and MFTA staff in Queens to document workflows, gather requirements, and identify pain points and opportunities in the manual donation process.

I led UX strategy, design, and front-end development for a new city-branded website and custom donation management portal, creating a more streamlined experience for donors, participants, staff, and warehouse volunteers.

</TextBlock>

## Notify NYC

Notify NYC, operated by New York City Emergency Management (NYCEM), is the city's official emergency communications program. The service provides residents and visitors with critical alerts, service updates, and emergency information.

### Public-facing website and admin portal

I led UX design and front-end development for both the public-facing website and the administrative platform used by NYCEM staff. My work focused on simplifying the enrollment experience for residents while designing tools that made it easier for staff to manage and publish emergency alerts.

</TextBlock>

## NYC DoRIS

The NYC Department of Records and Information Services (DoRIS) preserves the city's historical and government records. Its collection is an important resource for residents, researchers, historians, and the public.

</TextBlock>

<TextBlock>

### Order vital records

I led UX design for Order Vital Records, an online service that enables residents, researchers, and historians to request historical records and archival materials. Working closely with stakeholders and pairing with a React developer, I helped transform a complex government process into a guided digital experience.

The project focused on forms that changed based on the user's needs, helping people choose record types, understand eligibility requirements, provide supporting documents, and complete checkout.

</TextBlock>

## NYC PoleTop Manager

PoleTop Manager was a GIS-based telecommunications infrastructure management platform developed for the New York City Office of Technology and Innovation.

The system helped city staff and telecommunications franchise partners manage city-owned utility poles, infrastructure reservations, construction activity, and telecommunications deployments across all five boroughs.

I led UX strategy and front-end development, working closely with OTI stakeholders to turn specialized infrastructure workflows into a usable GIS platform. My work included dashboards, maps, asset views, and workflows for managing thousands of city-owned poles.

</TextBlock>

## Looking back

My time with the City shaped my passion for accessibility and inclusive design. It challenged me to think beyond individual screens and features and consider how digital services can better serve people from all backgrounds, abilities, and circumstances.

Many of the ideas that would eventually become the Natura11y framework took root during this time, particularly the importance of building accessibility into the foundation rather than treating it as an afterthought.

I'm grateful for the opportunity to work alongside city agencies and public servants in the greatest city in the world.

</TextBlock>
